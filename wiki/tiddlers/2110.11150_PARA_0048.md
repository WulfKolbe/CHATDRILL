Based on our theoretical insights, we have derived a parameter scaling strategy that enables pruning algorithms to find sparser strong lottery tickets.
We have extended the edge-popup algorithm {{2110.11150_REF_ramanujan2019whats||CIT}} for strong lottery ticket pruning accordingly and demonstrated the utility of our innovations on two case studies.
For imaging data, our nonzero bias initializations are well trainable, but the current generation of algorithms lacks the ability to draw an advantage over zero bias initialized networks (see App. (ref)), likely due to their parameter-inefficacy that has been reported before {{2110.11150_REF_fischer2022planting||CIT}}.
With the development of pruning algorithms that can find highly sparse strong lottery tickets, we anticipate that nonzero bias initializations are important for lottery ticket pruning in theory as well as practice.
