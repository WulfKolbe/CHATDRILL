Common initialization schemes (e.g. {{2110.11150_REF_HeInit||CIT}} {{2110.11150_REF_GlorotInit||CIT}} {{2110.11150_REF_dyniso||CIT}}) set all biases to zero, while network weights are drawn randomly to obtain parameter diversity. 
Proofs of the strong lottery ticket hypothesis have focused on this setting, thereby foregoing the universal approximation property of deep neural networks {{2110.11150_REF_scarselli1998universal||CIT}}, since pruning alone can only recover the zero-initialized biases.
