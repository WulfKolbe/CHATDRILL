The first proof of the strong lottery ticket hypothesis {{2110.11150_REF_malach2020proving||CIT}} has shown that a weight-bounded deep neural target network of depth {{2110.11150_FO0004||FO}} and width {{2110.11150_FO0036||FO}} with ReLU activation functions is contained up to error {{2110.11150_FO0034||FO}} with high probability in a larger deep neural network of double the depth of the target network, i.e., {{2110.11150_FO0064||FO}}.
Their strong requirement on the width of the large network to be of polynomial order {{2110.11150_FO0065||FO}} or, under additional sparsity assumptions, {{2110.11150_FO0066||FO}}, was subsequently improved to a logarithmic dependency of the form {{2110.11150_FO0067||FO}} for weights that follow an unusual hyperbolic distribution {{2110.11150_REF_orseau2020logarithmic||CIT}} and {{2110.11150_FO0068||FO}} for uniformly distributed weights {{2110.11150_REF_pensia2020optimal||CIT}}.
The improvement is achieved by the insight that many different parametrizations exist that can compute almost the same function as the target network.
All proofs have in common that two neural network layers are needed to approximate a neuron {{2110.11150_FO0069||FO}}, which explains the {{2110.11150_FO0064||FO}} depth requirement.
