Before we can answer how to initialize biases, we first have to face a different issue pertaining strong lottery tickets.
Standard initialization approaches, like He {{2110.11150_REF_HeInit||CIT}} or Glorot {{2110.11150_REF_GlorotInit||CIT}} initialization, achieve trainability by ensuring that the output of a deep neural network is contained within a reasonable range, thus rendering the computations of gradients numerically feasible. 
Network weights are commonly initialized according to a distribution with variance {{2110.11150_FO0035||FO}} that is inverse proportional to the number of neurons {{2110.11150_FO0036||FO}} in a layer, {{2110.11150_FO0037||FO}}. 
In consequence, after pruning a high percentage of these weights, the network output is heavily down-scaled, which needs to be compensated by up-scaling the output, as also discovered experimentally by {{2110.11150_REF_ramanujan2019whats||CIT}} and mentioned by {{2110.11150_REF_malach2020proving||CIT}}.
