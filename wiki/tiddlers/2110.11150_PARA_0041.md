We compare edge-popup with annealing of target sparsities against a version with scaling edge-popup-scaled for networks initialized with and without biases.
On two synthetic benchmarks, which turn out to be challenging settings for SLT pruning, we evaluate these methods for both He as well as `looks-linear' orthogonal initialization with and without nonzero bias extensions.
All experiments are carried out on commodity hardware.
Edge-popup training was conducted in the default way suggested by the original authors by SGD with momentum of {{2110.11150_FO0118||FO}} and weight decay {{2110.11150_FO0119||FO}}, combined with cosine annealing of the learning rate starting from {{2110.11150_FO0120||FO}}.
We train for {{2110.11150_FO0121||FO}} epochs and {{2110.11150_FO0122||FO}} annealing epochs in case of the shifted ReLU example and {{2110.11150_FO0123||FO}} annealing epochs in case of the ellipse and sparsity levels {{2110.11150_FO0124||FO}}.
In case of higher sparsity levels, {{2110.11150_FO0125||FO}} annealing epochs are sufficient. 
During annealing, we slowly reduce the sparsity over time by {{2110.11150_FO0126||FO}}, where {{2110.11150_FO0023||FO}} is the desired network sparsity, and {{2110.11150_FO0027||FO}} is the current epoch.
We used a batch size of {{2110.11150_FO0127||FO}} in all experiments and report mean based on {{2110.11150_FO0128||FO}} repetitions.
